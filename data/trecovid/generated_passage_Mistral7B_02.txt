MRNA vaccines are a type of vaccine that uses messenger RNA (mRNA) to teach cells how to produce a protein found on the surface of a virus. In the case of COVID-19, mRNA vaccines instruct cells to produce the spike protein found on the surface of the SARS-CoV-2 virus. The body recognizes this protein as foreign and mounts an immune response, producing antibodies that can fight the virus if the person is exposed in the future.  MRNA vaccines have several advantages over traditional vaccines. They can be developed more quickly because they don't require the growing and culturing of the virus or the production of large amounts of purified protein. Instead, they can be produced using synthetic biology techniques, allowing for rapid manufacturing and distribution.  MRNA vaccines also have a higher potential for efficacy because they can be designed to be more stable and to produce a stronger immune response than traditional vaccines.
